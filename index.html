Core-banking

Core banking systems (CBS) are the backend software powering a bank’s essential, real-time operations, including processing daily transactions, managing accounts, deposits, loans, and, says IBM, customer information. Modern solutions like Temenos and Thought Machine enable, say Vacuumlabs, cloud-native, API-driven, and scalable operations, allowing financial institutions to, according to Finastra, accelerate digital transformation and improve customer experience.

Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) system is a comprehensive framework of laws, regulations, and procedures designed to prevent criminals from disguising illegal funds as legitimate income. It requires financial institutions to monitor transactions, verify customer identities, and report suspicious activities to detect and stop financial crime. AML systems combat the laundering process—typically involving placement, layering, and integration—to prevent the misuse of the financial system for money laundering or terrorist financing.

Bill Payments

A bill payment system is a secure electronic service allowing individuals and businesses to pay recurring or one-time invoices—such as utilities, rent, or credit cards—directly from a bank account or through digital platforms. It automates payments, facilitates scheduling, and serves as a digital alternative to paper checks. The process usually involves the user adding a payee, inputting payment details, and the system initiating the transfer through the Automated Clearing House (ACH) or similar secure channels.
